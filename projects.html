Project 1

Salesforce BAU Governance & Release Control

Business problem
At Intrado Life & Safety, mission-critical Salesforce work was scattered across ServiceNow tickets, meetings, emails, and tribal knowledge. Production changes were hard to trace, auditors needed clearer evidence, and BAU support lacked a shared release model.
Solution
I drafted a Salesforce change-control and deployment process aligned with D365 and ServiceNow standards: ServiceNow intake, sandbox development, UAT sign-off, Gearset/Change Set promotion, production deployment ownership, emergency-change handling, rollback expectations, smoke testing, and audit evidence retention. I also organized recurring BAU themes around quoting, products, approvals, access, alerts, and integrations so triage had a repeatable path instead of one-off fixes.
Tools used
Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, Confluence, Gearset, Change Sets, Sandboxes, UAT
Outcome
Support work became easier to hand off, release ownership was clearer, and production changes followed a governed path that improved audit readiness and reduced unmanaged edits.
Project 2

Quote-to-Order & TaskRay Automation

Business problem
Quote synchronization, price books, product tiers, recurring fees, bundled products, approvals, and generated documents created sales friction. Downstream, TaskRay project creation from Salesforce orders failed partway through — users could create the project, but order-product mapping broke under record locking and unclear approval-versus-activation timing.
Solution
I traced quote behavior end to end: product setup, dynamic quote fields, approval logic, and document generation. On the TaskRay side, I reviewed Screen Flow steps, button visibility, project template selection, order-product association, and locking risk. The key decision was clarifying whether project creation should fire on order approval or order activation, then validating that behavior in UAT before any production change under change control.
Tools used
Sales Cloud, Apex, Flow Builder, Screen Flows, TaskRay, SOQL, Approvals, UAT
Outcome
Sales and implementation teams gained clearer quote-to-project behavior, automation changes became safer to promote, and BAU support had documented expected paths for troubleshooting.
Project 3

LexisNexis Salesforce Integration Readiness

Business problem
Salesforce used LexisNexis Accurint for due-diligence and risk screening, but Salesforce-side ownership was thin. Support needed a clear map of authentication, allowed users, search mappings, page layouts, triggers, batch jobs, alerts, and report visibility before production handoff.
Solution
I reviewed the Salesforce package configuration end to end: Named Credentials, allowed users, global settings, search mappings, Lightning page layouts and related lists, triggers, batch jobs, alerts, and reporting objects. I turned that review into support ownership notes, go-live readiness checks, and testing scenarios — while keeping credentials and secrets out of the documentation.
Tools used
Named Credentials, REST concepts, Apex, Batch Jobs, Permission Sets, Reports, Confluence
Outcome
The integration became supportable in BAU: ownership was clearer, authentication and mapping risk dropped, and handoff documentation reduced dependence on tribal knowledge.
Project 4

Smart Parking CRM & Operational Automation

Business problem
At Smart Park, reservation data, payment status, shuttle assignments, lot capacity, and customer issues lived in disconnected views. Agents and operations staff needed one CRM system to run bookings, service cases, and day-of coordination without chasing status across tools.
Solution
I built custom objects for reservations, customers, payments, shuttle assignments, and lot capacity, then layered Apex, triggers, validation rules, and record-triggered Flows for booking controls, status updates, notifications, and operational tasks. I designed agent Lightning pages and Service Cloud-style case workflows for payment issues, delays, cancellations, refunds, and escalations, and integrated payment, SMS, email, booking, and mapping systems through REST/JSON with validation and error logging. Reports and dashboards covered reservation volume, response time, payment status, shuttle activity, and recurring issue patterns.
Tools used
Salesforce CRM, Apex, Flow, Lightning Pages, REST APIs, JSON, Cases, Reports, Dashboards
Outcome
Operations ran from a single CRM view, failed integrations were easier to investigate, and customer issues moved through clearer case and escalation paths.
Project 5

Retail Loyalty & Campaign CRM

Business problem
At Clibase, retail, food service, pharmacy, and fitness clients were still running customers, orders, promotions, and loyalty from spreadsheets or disconnected tools. Owners needed structured CRM workflows and one place to see orders, payments, inventory signals, and campaign results.
Solution
I designed custom objects for customers, orders, products, promotions, loyalty rewards, inventory, and service requests. Apex triggers, SOQL, validation rules, and Flows handled profile updates, order tracking, promotion eligibility, loyalty points, and follow-up tasks. I integrated Salesforce with POS, web store, payment, and Messenger ordering systems via REST/JSON, then built reports and dashboards for sales trends, customer behavior, campaign performance, and retention decisions. Onboarding included field mapping, data import, duplicate review, and staff guidance.
Tools used
Apex, SOQL, Flow, Custom Objects, REST APIs, JSON, Reports, Dashboards, Data Loader
Outcome
Clients reduced duplicate entry, gained a unified customer and order view, and could run loyalty and promotions from configurable CRM rules instead of manual tracking.

Architecture pattern

Across these projects, I connect external platforms into governed Salesforce workflows — APIs, data validation, automation, security, observability, and release controls — so the CRM stays reliable as the business grows.
